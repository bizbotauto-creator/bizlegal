Consumer Protection

Consumer Protection Compliance Is Not Optional.

The Consumer Protection Act 68 of 2008 imposes far-reaching obligations on every business that supplies goods or services to consumers. Non-compliance exposes you to the National Consumer Tribunal, civil claims, and reputational damage. BizLegal helps you get — and stay — compliant.

What We Do

The CPA Applies to Almost Every Business in South Africa.

The Consumer Protection Act governs the relationship between suppliers and consumers across virtually every sector of the economy. From your standard terms and conditions to your returns policy and the way you market your products — the CPA has something to say about it. Most businesses have CPA exposure they are not aware of.

Broad Application

The CPA applies to any business that supplies goods or services to consumers in the ordinary course of business — regardless of size or industry.

Contracts & Terms Must Comply

Standard terms and conditions that are unfair, ambiguous or that limit consumer rights beyond what the CPA allows are unenforceable — and can attract regulatory action.

National Consumer Tribunal

The NCT can impose fines of up to R1 million or 10% of annual turnover (whichever is greater) for CPA violations. Complaints are also heard by the National Consumer Commission.

Terms & Conditions Review & Drafting

Review of your existing standard terms and conditions for CPA compliance, or drafting of compliant terms from scratch for your specific business model.

Returns, Refund & Warranty Policies

Your returns and refund policies must reflect the consumer rights guaranteed by section 56 of the CPA. We draft policies that are compliant and business-appropriate.

Marketing & Advertising Compliance

The CPA regulates promotional offers, loyalty programmes, advertising claims and direct marketing. We review your marketing practices for CPA compliance.

Consumer Complaint Procedures

We establish a compliant internal consumer complaint procedure, reducing the likelihood of NCC referrals and demonstrating good faith compliance to the regulator.

Supply Chain & Supplier Agreements

CPA obligations run through supply chains. We review your supplier agreements to ensure that liability is appropriately allocated and that your business is protected.

Cooling-Off & Cancellation Rights

The CPA grants consumers specific cooling-off and cancellation rights. We ensure your contracts and procedures correctly accommodate these rights.

Staff Training on CPA Obligations

Customer-facing staff need to understand consumer rights under the CPA. We deliver practical training tailored to your industry and business model.
